# Space Trip V1 — Generation Prompts

All six assets used Codex's built-in OpenAI image-generation route. Every
prompt prohibited text, branding, flags, insignia, real agencies, copied
mission hardware and additional people.

## ST-001 — Maya EVA hero

**References:** canonical Maya image; V4 composition frame; unbranded astronaut
source.

> Create a photorealistic 16:9 personal space-travel photograph featuring the
> same fictional Maya Chen as the astronaut. Preserve the V4 layout: restrained
> stars, recognisably blue-green Earth entering lower-left, small grey Moon high
> in frame and an obvious warm-white Sun fully inside upper-right. Maya floats
> slightly right of centre in an original neutral white civilian EVA suit, one
> knee bent, arms relaxed. Her dark reflective visor makes the Face unsuitable
> for matching while the full articulated Body remains visible. Use plausible
> materials, warm solar rim, cool Earth bounce and quiet intimacy. No text,
> reticle, flash, UI, marks, flags, brands, copied mission hardware or extra
> people.

## ST-002 — Maya suit-up

**References:** canonical Maya image; ST-001.

> Create a photorealistic portrait 2:3 personal travel photograph of exactly
> the same Maya preparing for the same trip. Preserve her face, age, East Asian
> features and shoulder-length black hair. She stands naturally in the same
> neutral white suit in an understated civilian preparation room, helmet under
> one arm, clear unobstructed Face and a grounded smile. Use candid phone-camera
> texture and soft warm practical light with cool window fill. Avoid catalogue
> polish, glamour retouching, identity drift and all marks or text.

## ST-003 — Maya at the capsule window

**References:** canonical Maya image; ST-001; ST-002.

> Create a photorealistic landscape 3:2 travel photograph of the same Maya
> secured inside the same generic orbital capsule. She wears the same suit and
> helmet. The clear visor is lowered but a broad window reflection crosses part
> of her Face, leaving her human-recognisable but weaker evidence than ST-002.
> Show a secondary Earth window, cool Earth light, warm cabin practicals and
> believable handheld imperfection. Preserve identity and suit continuity; no
> opaque visor, marks, text, extra people or science-fiction armour.

## ST-004 — Rear-view Earth window

**References:** canonical Maya image; ST-001; ST-002.

> Create a photorealistic portrait 2:3 travel photograph of the same Maya from
> completely behind inside the orbital observation bay. She floats gently in
> the same suit, full Body readable from helmet to boots, one gloved hand on a
> rail, looking toward blue Earth. Her Face is completely hidden and no
> reflection reveals it. Use cool Earth outline, warm cabin fill and personal
> imperfect framing. Preserve anatomical and suit continuity; no marks, text
> or publicity-poster styling.

## ST-005 — Maya's orbital sunrise POV

**References:** ST-001; ST-003.

> Create a photorealistic landscape 3:2 photograph taken by Maya from her own
> point of view. No person or identifiable body part appears, so Maya can be
> known Present without an honest Face, Head or Body region. Through the same
> capsule window, show a blue Earth horizon at orbital sunrise, restrained
> stars, subtle glass reflection, a small generic ceramic cup and a clipped
> blank notebook floating nearby. No photographer reflection, readable text,
> marks, fantasy planet or visible astronaut.
